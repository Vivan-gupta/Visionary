Lidar

Lidar, which stands for Light Detection and Ranging, is a remote sensing method that uses light in the form of a pulsed laser to measure variable distances to the Earth. This technology generates precise, three-dimensional information about the shape of the Earth and its surface characteristics. Lidar is widely used in geography, archaeology, forestry, geology, seismology, atmospheric physics, and autonomous vehicles for creating detailed topographical maps and for navigation.
